Difference between Desert and Temperate Forest Ecosystem


Aspect Desert Ecosystem Temperate Forest Ecosystem
Climate Hot and arid with extreme temperature ranges Moderate with distinct seasons (spring, summer, fall, winter)
Precipitation Very low, often less than 250 mm per year Moderate to high, varying from 500 to 2,500 mm annually
Vegetation Sparse and adapted to conserve water (cacti, succulents) Diverse with deciduous and evergreen trees, shrubs, and various plant species
Biodiversity Limited due to harsh conditions Higher biodiversity due to more favorable conditions
Adaptations Plants and animals adapted to conserve water and withstand heat Adaptations for seasonal changes and temperature fluctuations
Soil Type Often sandy and lacks organic matter Varied soil types with more organic content and nutrients
Animal Life Species adapted to survive with limited water (camels, reptiles) Diverse fauna including mammals, birds, and insects
Human Impact Vulnerable to habitat degradation and desertification Faces threats like deforestation and habitat loss due to human activities
