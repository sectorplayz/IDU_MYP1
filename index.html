Ecosystem






An ecosystem refers to a dynamic community of living organisms that interact with each other and their surroundings. These communities can exist on land or water and can vary in size from small to large. Each organism within the ecosystem has a unique role in the food chain, contributing to the maintenance of balance. Some of the most well-known examples of ecosystems are forests, coral reefs, and grasslands. Ecosystems play a crucial role in preserving biodiversity and sustaining all forms of life on Earth.


Classifications of Ecosystems



Ecosystem can be classified into the following categories:-

  1. Terrestrial Ecosystem

  2. Aerial Ecosystem

  3. Aquatic Ecosystem




Heat Zones of the world






The Frigid Zone, Temperate Zone, and Torrid Zone are the three heat zones on the planet. The Torrid Zone is just the hottest of the different zones since it is closest to the equator. The Temperate Zone can be broken into two zones along either side of the Torrid Zone and has temperatures in the range from warm to cool. There are two Frigid Zones, one at the North Pole and another at the South Pole. The Frigid Zone is indeed the coolest of the three zones, with polar night and midnight sun for a portion of the year.
