Terrestrial Ecosystem



Terrestrial ecosystems are communities of plants, animals, and microorganisms that live on land in different habitats. Plants are the primary producers, while herbivores, carnivores, and decomposers make up the food chain. Each ecosystem has unique environmental conditions that determine the organisms that can survive and thrive. Human activities, such as deforestation and pollution, can have a significant impact on these ecosystems. It is important to protect them to ensure their long-term health and survival.
Examples of terrestrial animals include cats, ants, dogs, raccoons, spiders, kangaroos, tigers, lions, mice, bats, bulls, oxen, leopards, elephants, and many more
